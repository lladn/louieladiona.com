I am **Louie Ladiona**, a Site Reliability Engineer (SRE) with a strong focus on cloud computing and distributed systems. I build practical tools to solve real-world operational challenges, with an emphasis on reliability, scalability, and automation. Well sometimes I spend most of my time on my neovim configuration. I actively explore and work with open-source technologies, not just using them, but actually digging into how they work, customizing them, and sometimes contributing back. There's something satisfying about understanding a tool deeply enough to bend it to exactly what you need.


## Hobbies 
Running, hiking, and biking are my main outlets. There's something about being outside and moving that resets everything, and it's hard to stay stressed when you're halfway up a trail or pushing through the last kilometer of a run. I find I do some of my best thinking during these moments, when I'm not actively trying to think.
Walking fits into this too, but in a quieter way. I walk without a destination sometimes, just to let my mind wander. It's become a kind of thinking ritual. The ideas that show up on a walk are usually the ones I wasn't able to force at a desk.

I play a lot of video games across a pretty wide range of genres — RPGs, roguelikes, card games, soulslikes, FPS. I'm drawn to games that have depth and punish complacency, the kind where dying means you actually learned something. Roguelikes and soulslikes scratch that itch particularly well and sometimes it's exhausting  also. 

Coffee is a whole separate thing. I don't just drink it — I enjoy it deliberately, especially on weekend nights. A quiet cup while reading something interesting or going down a rabbit hole on a new idea is one of my favorite ways to spend an evening. It's less about the caffeine and more about the ritual.
